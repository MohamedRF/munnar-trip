Lakkam Waterfalls Open Maps Bathing
Entry ₹50/person · Opens 8:30 AM. Spend full 2.5 hrs bathing in the natural pool — stones are slippery, walk barefoot on visible rocks. Forest trek ₹100/person runs alongside the fall (~1 hr). Water in April is ice-cold but swimmable. Café on site for a snack after your dip.
→ ~5 km drive · 10 min toward Thoovanam

Thoovanam Waterfall View Point Open Maps Viewpoint
A stunning hidden viewpoint overlooking a tiered waterfall deep in the Chinnar forest zone. Quick 30-minute stop — photograph from the viewing platform. May require a short 200m forest walk from the road.
→ ~3 km · Sandalwood Forest area
3
12:00 – 12:30 — SANDALWOOD FOREST
Marayoor Sandalwood Forest Open Maps Forest
Marayoor is home to India's only naturally occurring wild sandalwood forest — a unique and rare sight. The forest department allows accompanied walks. Entry with guide is ₹50–100. The scent in the grove is something special — don't skip this.
→ ~35 km via NH49 · ~55 min to Munnar direction · Anamudi viewpoint on the way
4
13:30 – 14:00 — ANAMUDI VIEW POINT
Anamudi View Point Open Maps View
Roadside viewpoint offering a glimpse of Anamudi peak (2,695 m) — the highest peak in South India. Quick 20-minute stop. Good photo spot with the peak framed by tea slopes. On the Munnar bypass road.
5
14:05 – 14:25 — 8TH MILE MUNNAR
8th Mile, Munnar Open Maps Scenic Stop
A popular scenic stretch on the Munnar highway — tea gardens carpeting both sides with the hill valleys opening below. Quick roadside stop for photos. Small shops for fresh lemon tea available.

Munnar Town · Restaurant Find on Maps Lunch
Good options in Munnar town: Rapsy Restaurant or East End Hotel for Kerala meals (rice + fish curry + papad + payasam). Budget ~₹250/person. This is also your last town for ATMs and essentials before Viripara route.
🔀 Alternate Route for Viripara: After lunch, take the Anavilasom road instead of the main Munnar–Kothamangalam highway to reach Viripara Waterfalls by 4 PM, then loop back via 2nd Mile & Karadipara toward Candlewood. Open alternate route ↗

A multi-tiered cascade nestled in a forest clearing — less crowded than Lakkam. Reach before 4 PM to have enough light. Short trek from roadside parking (~500m). Swimming possible near the base pool. Return via alternate route back toward Munnar.

Day 2
Mattupetty → Top Station → Vattavada Direction
Apr 4 Sat
🌿 Full-day route: Flower Garden → Photo Point → Mattupetty Dam → Shooting Point → Echo Point → Kundala Dam → Top Station (→ optionally continue to Vattavada village if time permits). Start by 8:30 AM sharp to beat weekend crowds at Top Station.
1
08:30 – 09:00 — FLOWER GARDEN + PHOTO POINT
Flower Garden & Photo Point, Munnar Find on Maps Gardens
The KTDC Flower Garden is near Munnar town — manicured hedges and flower beds. Just 2 km further is the famous Tea Garden Photo Point for the iconic wide-angle tea estate shot. 30 minutes combined.
2
09:00 – 09:30 — ELEPHANT SAFARI / ELEPHANT ARRIVAL SPOT
Elephant Arrival Spot · Mattupetty Road Find on Maps Wildlife
Around 9 AM elephants from the Mattupetty forest area are sometimes seen crossing near the road. The jeep safari operator (booked via Candlewood) will guide you to active spots. Elephant rides are not recommended — spotting from safe distance is the experience here.
3
09:30 – 10:15 — TEA FACTORY VISIT
Tata Tea / KDHP Tea Factory, Munnar Find on Maps Factory Tour
The KDHP (Kannan Devan Hills Plantations) museum and tea factory near Mattupetty road offers 30-min guided tours (₹100/person) showing the full tea-making process from leaf to pack. Buy fresh loose-leaf Munnar tea at the end.
4
10:15 – 11:30 — MATTUPETTY DAM + BOTANICAL GARDEN
Mattupetty Dam Open Maps Boating
Speed boat ₹1,100 for 5 seats / 15 min — thrilling ride on the calm reservoir. The nearby Indo-Swiss Botanical Garden (managed by the farm) has a pleasant morning walk through high-altitude plant collections. Grab a coffee from the dam side stalls.
5
11:30 – 12:00 — SHOOTING POINT
Shooting Point, Mattupetty Find on Maps Viewpoint
Famous film shooting location with a wide grassy meadow framed by tea hills and distant peaks. One of the most photographed spots in Munnar — great group photos. 30-minute stop. Horse rides available (₹200/ride).
6
12:00 – 12:45 — ECHO POINT
Echo Point Open Maps Echo + Boating
Shout into the valley and hear your voice echo back from the hills — genuinely fun! Entry ₹10. Boating and kayaking on Kundala lake arm (₹50–150). The scenery at midday when mist clears is spectacular. 45 minutes here.
🍽
12:45 – 13:30 — LUNCH
Roadside restaurant near Echo Point / Kundala Find on Maps Lunch
Small eateries near the Echo Point parking sell Kerala meals, noodles and maggi. Eat a light-medium lunch — Top Station involves some walking. Carry water (no shops at Top Station summit).
7
13:45 – 14:30 — KUNDALA DAM + LAKE
Kundala Dam & Lake Open Maps Shikara Boating
Serene high-altitude lake with Kashmiri-style shikara boats and pedal boats. Carry cash — no mobile signal here. The forest edge around the lake sometimes has sambar deer in the afternoon. 40-minute stop.
★
14:30 – 16:30 — TOP STATION (6,300 ft)
Top Station, Kerala–Tamil Nadu Border Find on Maps Crown Jewel
Don't skip this. 44 km from Munnar town, 1 hr drive from Kundala. Walk ~1 km down a trail from the road for the full valley panorama — the Western Ghats fall away dramatically to the Tamil Nadu plains below. Views into three districts. On clear afternoons (April is usually clear), the view stretches 80–100 km. Allow 1.5–2 hrs here.
9
16:30 – 17:30 — VATTAVADA VILLAGE (if time allows)
Vattavada Village Find on Maps Village
15 km beyond Top Station on the Bodimettu road. A high-altitude vegetable farming village — carrots, beets, cabbage grown at 7,000 ft. The drive through the valley is stunning. If skipping, head directly back toward Devikulam for check-in.
🌿
17:30 – 18:00 — GOV. BOTANICAL GARDEN (optional)
Government Botanical Garden, Munnar Open Maps Optional
Only if energy permits on the return. A maintained garden with exotic hill plants and a rose garden. Closes at 6 PM. Skip if tired and head straight to Mount Elbrus.

~18:30 — NIGHT STAY · DAY 2
Mount Elbrus Homestay, Devikulam Open Maps Stay ★ 5.0
Laksham Colony, Devikulam. Rated a perfect 5 stars — peaceful, beautiful location near Munnar. Take a good rest tonight. Tomorrow is an early sunrise start. Ask the host for the fastest route to Door of Heaven.

05:30 – 06:30 — DOOR OF HEAVEN (SUNRISE)
Door of Heaven Open Maps 🌅 Sunrise
Leave by 5:15 AM — 5 min drive from Mount Elbrus. A dramatic narrow gap between two hillsides frames the sunrise horizon perfectly. Best viewpoint in the area for golden hour. Cold at dawn — carry a light jacket. Return by 6:30 AM for breakfast.

07:00 – 08:00 — BATH + BREAKFAST AT MOUNT ELBRUS
Mount Elbrus Homestay · Checkout
Freshen up, have breakfast (request the host to arrange the night before), pack bags and checkout. Today is a long exploration day followed by the night drive home.
1
08:15 – 09:00 — SKYFALL VIEWPOINT
SkyFall Viewpoint Open Maps Cliff View
A dramatic cliff-edge viewpoint south of Devikulam with a near-vertical drop to a valley. Morning light here is excellent. Walk ~300m from road. Stay behind safety barriers — the drop is sheer. 40-minute stop.
2
09:15 – 10:00 — LOCKHART GAP VIEW
Lockhart Gap Road View Open Maps Valley View
A famous gap in the Cardamom Hills ridge that frames a long valley corridor. Named after the British-era Lockhart road. Morning mist typically still lingers at this elevation at 9 AM — ethereal views. 30–40 minute stop.
→ ~25 km toward Anayirankal area · 40 min drive
3
10:45 – 11:15 — ANAYIRANGAL VIEW POINT
Anayirangal View Point Open Maps Panorama
A large open viewpoint overlooking the Anayirankal reservoir below and the forested ridges of the Periyar Tiger Reserve in the distance. Clear mornings give spectacular layered ridge views. 30 minutes.
4
11:20 – 12:00 — ELEPHANT ABODE BOATING CENTER
Elephant Abode Boating Center Open Maps Boating
Motorboat and rowing boat rides on the Anayirankal reservoir with views of the forested shoreline. The area is also a known elephant corridor — keep your eyes on the tree line. 40 minutes including the boat ride.
5
12:00 – 12:30 — ANAYIRANKAL DAM
Anayirankal Dam Open Maps Dam View
A concrete dam holding back the Anayirankal lake — walk along the dam wall for views of both the reservoir and the forested valley below. Very quiet and uncrowded. 20-minute stop, adjacent to Elephant Abode.
🍽
12:30 – 13:15 — LUNCH
Restaurant · Anayirankal / Bodimettu Road Find on Maps Lunch
Small local eateries along the Bodimettu road. Kerala meals or fried rice available. Eat well — you have a ~5 hr drive to Chennai tonight. Carry water and snacks for the return journey.
→ ~20 km toward Bodimettu · 35 min drive on forest road
6
13:45 – 14:30 — BODIMETTU VIEW POINT
Bodimettu View Point Open Maps Afternoon View
A commanding hilltop viewpoint at the Kerala–Tamil Nadu border ridge. On a clear afternoon you can see the plains of Tamil Nadu stretching far below. The road up is narrow but manageable in the Urban Cruiser. 40-minute stop.
→ ~20 km toward Bodinayakanur via Anai Pillaiyar temple road
7
15:00 – 16:00 — ANAI PILLAIYAR KOVIL BATHING POINT
Anai Pillaiyar Kovil Bathing Point Open Maps Bathing + Temple
A river bathing point below an ancient hill temple — the water flows down from the Munnar hills and is cool and clear. Take a refreshing bath before the long drive home. The temple itself (Lord Ganesh in an elephant form) is worth a quick visit. Last chance to freshen up before the return drive.
→ ~15 km to Bodinayakanur town · 25 min · Fuel up here for Chennai drive

Petrol Bunk + Dinner · Bodinayakanur Find on Maps
Full tank here — last major town before the highway. Have a good dinner (~6–7 PM). Bodinayakanur has several hotels on the main road. Stock water, snacks and coffee for the overnight drive. Rest in the car for 30 min before departure.

Essential Tips for the Road
⛽ Fuel: Fill up at Udumalaipettai (night), Munnar town (Day 1), and Bodinayakanur (return)
💧 Water: Carry 5L per person at all times — ghat roads have no shops
📶 Network: Airtel/BSNL works best in Munnar hills. Kundala and Top Station have weak/no signal
💵 Cash: ATMs in Munnar town only. Carry ₹3,000–5,000 cash for entries and boat rides
🥾 Footwear: Waterproof sandals for waterfalls, sports shoes for viewpoint walks
🧥 Clothing: Carry a light jacket — sunrise spots (Door of Heaven, Top Station) are 10–14°C
🌿 Leeches: Apply Dettol/Savlon on ankles before forest walks at Lakkam and Thoovanam
